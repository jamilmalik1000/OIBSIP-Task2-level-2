Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

introduction

Quaid-e-Azam is a title given to Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founding
father of Pakistan. Born on December 25, 1876, in Karachi (now in
Pakistan), Jinnah was a prominent political leader, statesman, and
lawyer. He played a crucial role in the creation of Pakistan and
served as its first Governor-General until his death on September 11, 1948.

Contribution For Pakistan

  • Instrumental in the creation of Pakistan as a separate Muslim state.
  • Fought for the rights and representation of Muslims in British India.
  • Leadership of the All India Muslim League united Muslims under a common cause.
  • Advocated the Two-Nation Theory, emphasizing separate identities for Hindus and Muslims.
  • Negotiated with British authorities to secure Pakistan's independence.
  • Served as Pakistan's first Governor-General.
  • Laid the foundation for a democratic and inclusive nation.
